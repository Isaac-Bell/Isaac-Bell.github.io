Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

"'He who says he can and he who says he can't are both usually right " - Confucius. Neuroplascity is the idea that the brain can learn and form and strengthen new connections as opposed to being static and unchanging. For years the idea of intelligence has been "either you've got it or you don't". Some innate attribute that some people are born with, or not... Going through life, studies have shown that people who believe that they arn't very intelligent for whatever reason will not as readily take on new challenges and therfore new opportunities. Neuroplacity says that those people instead of being static with either having intelligence or not, can actually grow and learn in any skill they put their mind too. The studies have shown that new information is recieved as real physical "real-estate" in the brain that looks like a small ball, and that when called upon will be strengthened with proteins between the trees that are calling them. The more that this new information is used, the more the new cell will resemble a tree and be connected to other cells within the brain, creating a network of understanding. People who understand the science of the brain can understand that although a task or a new skill might be a big task to learn, it is possible with enough practise!

Discuss how might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit.

If i can remember that my brain needs practise, and repitition to solidy new ideas and information, i think it will remind me to give myself the patience that i need when i don't understand. Often if i'm learning something new, i want to understand it quickly, and if i don't , sometimes i can move on. But understanding this function of the brain brings about a patient hope that given the right conditions i will understand and can be really great!

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity.

Learn more and learn often! The brain is a muscle and the more you fire the neurons through deliberate practise and learning of new things the faster it can form and strengthen new connections.
